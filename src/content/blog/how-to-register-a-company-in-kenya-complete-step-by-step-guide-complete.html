How to Register a Company in Kenya: Essential Steps

Embarking on the path of entrepreneurship in Kenya requires a solid start, and that begins with the official company registration process. The process is streamlined through the Business Registration Service via the eCitizen portal, ensuring that business owners can register their companies efficiently and compliantly. Here, we'll guide you through the critical steps of company registration in Kenya.

Setting Up an eCitizen Account

First, create an account on the eCitizen platform. This portal is the gateway for all government services related to business registration in Kenya, including obtaining your KRA PIN, which is mandatory for all directors and shareholders. You'll need to provide personal details such as your ID number, email, and a phone number.

Reserving Your Company Name

Once your eCitizen account is active, proceed to reserve your company name through the Business Registration Service. You can submit up to three names in order of preference, with a minimal reservation fee of KES 150. This step is crucial as it confirms the uniqueness of your business name in the Kenyan market. The name reservation and initial business registration steps are now integrated, simplifying the process.

Preparing and Submitting Incorporation Documents

Next, prepare the required documents, including the CR1, CR2, CR8 forms, and the Statement of Nominal Share Capital. These forms are available for download from the eCitizen portal. Ensure you also have digital copies of all directors/shareholders' National IDs or passports, along with their KRA PINs. Foreign nationals must provide certified passport copies and their PIN certificate.

Pro Tip: Always double-check your document submissions for accuracy to avoid delays in the registration process. For more insights on tax obligations post-registration, visit our KRA tax guide.

Submitting your incorporation documents online is the final step. After paying the registration fee, which ranges between KES 10,650 and KES 10,750, your application will be processed, typically within 5 to 7 business days. Once approved, you will receive your Certificate of Incorporation along with a CR12 form, which lists all directors and shareholders.

With your company now officially registered, you can focus on the next steps, such as obtaining necessary business permits in Kenya to operate legally and effectively.

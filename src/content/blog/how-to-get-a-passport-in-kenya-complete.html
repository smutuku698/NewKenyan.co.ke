Required Documents for Passport Application in Kenya

Securing a passport in Kenya involves gathering a specific set of documents to ensure a smooth application process. Whether you're a first-time applicant or looking to renew or replace your passport, understanding and compiling these documents is your first crucial step.

Documents Needed for First-Time Applicants

First-time passport applicants must provide a comprehensive list of documents to support their identity and eligibility. This includes:

  • A completed application Form 19, which must be filled online and then printed.
  • Original and photocopy of your birth certificate and national ID card (for applicants aged 18 and above).
  • Three recent passport-sized photos with a white background.
  • Supporting documents such as parents' ID cards or death certificates, and a consent letter for minors.

Additional Requirements for Passport Renewal or Replacement

For those renewing or replacing a passport, the requirements include:

  • Your previous passport and a duly filled application Form 19 for renewals.
  • For lost passports: a police abstract, sworn affidavit, and personal statement.
  • For damaged passports: the damaged passport along with a sworn affidavit and personal statement.

Company Registration Kenya: Complete Guide

Introduction to Company Registration in Kenya

Starting a business in Kenya has become significantly easier with the digitization of company registration processes. The Kenya government has streamlined business registration through the eCitizen portal, making it possible to register your company entirely online.

In 2025, registering a company in Kenya involves several key steps, from choosing your business name to obtaining necessary permits. This guide will walk you through every detail you need to know.

How to Register a Company in Kenya: Essential Steps

Step 1: Choose Your Business Name

Your business name must be unique and not conflict with existing registered companies. The Registrar of Companies will verify name availability during the registration process.

  • Name should reflect your business activity
  • Avoid names too similar to existing companies
  • Cannot use restricted words without permission
  • Must end with "Limited" or "Ltd" for private companies

Step 2: Access the eCitizen Portal

Visit https://ecitizen.go.ke and create an account if you don't have one. You'll need:

  • Valid email address
  • Mobile phone number
  • National ID or Passport number

Step 3: Select Business Registration Services

Navigate to "Business Registration Services" and choose "Company Registration" from the available options.

Step 4: Fill Out the Application Form

Complete all required fields including:

  • Proposed company name (3 alternatives recommended)
  • Business address
  • Directors' information
  • Share capital details
  • Business activity description

Legal Requirements Post-Registration

After successful company registration, you must comply with several legal requirements:

Within 30 Days:

  • Obtain business permit from county government
  • Register with KRA for tax purposes
  • Register employees with NSSF
  • Register for NHIF

Ongoing Requirements:

  • File annual returns
  • Maintain proper books of accounts
  • Hold annual general meetings
  • Comply with tax filing obligations

Tax Obligations for Kenyan Businesses

Understanding your tax obligations is crucial for business success:

Key Taxes to Know:

  • Corporate Income Tax: 30% on company profits
  • VAT: 16% on goods and services (if turnover exceeds KES 5 million)
  • PAYE: On employee salaries
  • Withholding Tax: On various payments to residents and non-residents

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to register a company in Kenya?

Company registration typically takes 7-14 business days through the eCitizen portal, provided all documents are complete and accurate.

What is the minimum share capital required?

There's no minimum share capital requirement for private companies in Kenya. You can start with as little as KES 100.

Can foreigners register a company in Kenya?

Yes, foreigners can register companies in Kenya. However, some sectors have restrictions on foreign ownership.

Do I need a lawyer to register a company?

While not mandatory, consulting a lawyer or company secretary is recommended, especially for complex business structures.
